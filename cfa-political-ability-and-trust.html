It can be seen that all indicators have relatively high loadings on their respective latent factors, the lowest of which being actrolga with a loading of 0.5226, thereby indicating that the latent factors of this measurement model are adequately accounting for the correlation in the data.

We then reconsider the modification indices, in Table . From this, we add the indicator trstep into the regression relationship with social trust and add the residual covariance . The cross-loading of trstep onto social trust is interesting because there certainly could be a directional relationship between the extent to which people trust in institutions such as the European Union and the extent to which they trust in others. We again refit the model, and show the estimates in Table . It can be seen that the cross-loading for trstep on the social trust latent factor is small and negative. We take this to mean that while there is an interesting relationship between trust in the European parliament and social trust, it is not correctly represented in the specification of the measurement model here, and potentially could be part of a structural relationship.

The fit measures of the original 3-factor model and the two modified models are shown in Table . While both modified models provide a much better fit than the original, the second set of modifications appears to make only a marginal difference. Given the small negative loading of trstep on social trust, we proceed with the model with only the first set of modifications. This model will be used in our mediation analysis.
